\subsubsection*{Problem Statement Question 2}
A biomechanics team models a spherical joint system with radius \textbf{7.5 units}, where the \textbf{joint force density} at a point $(x, y, z)$ is described by:
\[ I(x,y,z) = 600.17x^{2} + 600.17y^{2} + 600.17z^{2} \]
Compute the total joint force in the system using \textbf{spherical coordinates}.

\subsubsection*{Solution}
The total joint force $F$ is defined as the triple integral of the density function $I(x, y, z)$ over the spherical region $E$:
\[ F = \iiint_E I(x, y, z) \, dV \]

First, we convert the density function and the region boundaries into spherical coordinates. The transformation is given by $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = \rho^2$. Thus, the density function becomes:
\[ I(\rho, \phi, \theta) = 600.17\rho^2 \]

The volume element in spherical coordinates is $dV = \rho^2 \sin\phi \, d\rho \, d\phi \, d\theta$. Since the region $E$ is a full sphere of radius $7.5$, the limits of integration are:
\[ 0 \le \rho \le 7.5, \quad 0 \le \phi \le \pi, \quad 0 \le \theta \le 2\pi \]

Applying the iterated integration process step-by-step:
\[ F = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} \int_{0}^{7.5} 600.17 \rho^4 \sin\phi \, d\rho \, d\phi \, d\theta \]

\vspace{1cm}
\noindent\textbf{Step 1: Integrate with respect to $\rho$.}

Treating $\phi$ and $\theta$ as constants, we integrate the innermost function:
\[ \int_{0}^{7.5} 600.17 \rho^4 \sin\phi \, d\rho = 600.17 \sin\phi \left[ \frac{\rho^5}{5} \right]_{0}^{7.5} \]
\[ = 600.17 \sin\phi \left( \frac{151875}{32} \right) \approx 2848463.0859 \sin\phi \]
\[ \Rightarrow F = \int_{0}^{2\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi} 2848463.0859 \sin\phi \, d\phi \, d\theta \]

\vspace{1cm}
\noindent\textbf{Step 2: Integrate with respect to $\phi$.}

Next, we integrate the resulting expression with respect to $\phi$:
\[ \int_{0}^{\pi} 2848463.0859 \sin\phi \, d\phi = 2848463.0859 [-\cos\phi]_{0}^{\pi} \]
Substituting the bounds gives:
\[ = 2848463.0859 (-\cos\pi + \cos0) = 5696926.1718 \]
The integral expression simplifies to:
\[ F = \int_{0}^{2\pi} 5696926.1718 \, d\theta \]

\vspace{1cm}
\noindent\textbf{Step 3: Integrate with respect to $\theta$.}

Finally, we evaluate the outermost integral with respect to $\theta$:
\[ \int_{0}^{2\pi} 5696926.1718 \, d\theta = 5696926.1718 [\theta]_{0}^{2\pi} = 11393852.3437\pi \]

\vspace{1cm}
\noindent\textbf{Step 4: Simplify the result.}

Performing the final arithmetic calculation:
\[ F \approx \boxed{35,794,842.8185} \]

Thus, the total joint force distributed in the spherical system is exactly $\mathbf{35,794,842.8185}$.
